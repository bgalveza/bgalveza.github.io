Research Interests

Thesis: Homotopical and Cohomological Methods for the Study of Singularities
(Méthodes homotopiques et cohomologiques pour l’étude des singularités)

My research lies at the intersection of algebraic topology and singular geometry. I am interested in addressing geometric questions of singular spaces by employing techniques from abstract homotopy theory, including ∞-categories , stable homotopy theory, and six functor formalism.

Specifically, my work focuses on the homotopy theory of stratified spaces. I aim to rehabilitate the "missing" tangent bundle on singular spaces by developing a theory of stratified vector bundles and bivariant intersection homology[cite: 44, 105]. The goal is to build a robust geometric framework—compatible with intersection cohomology—that allows for the construction of characteristic classes, orientations, and Riemann-Roch theorems in the stratified setting[cite: 36, 44].
